A Guide to Manchester

This is a complete guide of everything you need to know about living in Manchester. If you are trying new foods, visiting new places or maybe trying to save money this guide has everything for you!

What can you do?

Best resturants

Manchester is home to a melting pot of cultures and as result, has become a hotspot for heavenly cuisine. In the city centre, you can find the UK’s second largest Chinatown - which has dozens of exceptional restaurants serving delicious sweet and savoury food. Manchester is also known for the curry mile as it is the UK’s largest concentration of south asian restaurants, here you can feast on a range of foods rich in flavour and spice.

Study Areas

As a student, you will find yourself spending ample amounts of time studying, so why not study in some of Manchester’s historic libraries or modern study spaces. So grab yourself a coffee and make your way to one of these locations and ace those exams.

Groceries

For most students budgeting whilst at university is a must, and grocery shopping is no exception to this. It can be difficult to navigate an unfamiliar city whilst trying to bring in your weekly shopping, so we have included a list below of some budget friendly shops that are local to student areas.

Shopping

Manchester has a lot to offer for everyone, especially when it comes to shopping. Whether you are buying a gift or buying for yourself, there are plenty of places in Manchester that you can visit. Below we have recommended some popular shopping centres, but don't let this limit you from exploring all of the amazing shops that Manchester has to offer.

Coffee Places

For those that need some coffee in the morning or whilst studying, below are the most unique coffee places in all of Manchester. It will increase your productivity and give you a good start to the day. These coffee shops serve a range of hot drinks from a regular cup of coffee to a hot cup of matcha tea.



Transport Around Manchester

Stagecoach


For some students buses are the most popular mode of transport, For those that live off campus, take advantage of the Uniday rider to get to those important lectures



Tram



A convenient way for travelling across the city and by using the touch in/out method travelling, is stress free and simple. Alternatively, you can book tickets online or at a cahier on the platform.



Train


For students that live off campus/ have to commute, trains can be a very effective way of travelling to campus. Trains are usually a quicker form of transportation compared to other public transports, as well as cheaper ticket prices (if you own a rail card).


Cycling

Goverment schemes such as Beryl makes cycling in manchester much more accessible than ever before. Simply download the app and find your nearest beryl bike and cycle your way through town



Walking

Though manchester is busling city with various modes of transports you can also take a stroll with some paths being built specifically for students





Uber

Whether you’re exploring the city with friends or coming home from a night out, taxi services such as Uber can be very reliable transportation. Simply by downloading the app, you book a taxi journey anywhere around the city. Local services are also available and can be contacted via calling.
