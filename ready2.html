The University of Manchester Getting Ready Guide

Whether you're living in private accommodation, University halls or commuting from home this year we've got you covered.

Explore your options and different types of accommodation in Manchester. Once you’ve organised your living arrangements check out our useful moving in checklist. Here you'll also find more about the ResLife team in halls of residence and our Living at Home society to help get you settled. We've also got some information about staying safe in your local area and how to be a valued member of your new community.

Accommodation for students

Halls of residence.

The University's huge range of accommodation means we have got something for all tastes and budgets You can apply for University accommodation by completing an online application.Applying is free but if you wish to make to accept an offer accommodation you will need to make an advance license fee of 400 pounds to secure your room. you will need a valid email address to complete the application and a valid University of Manchester ID number which will be on your offer letter. if you change your mind about where you will be living, it is okay becauase your money is refundable!!

Private Accommodation.

There is plenty of private accommodation acroos the city if you do not fancy living in university halls. Manchester student homes can provide details of privately rented properties, independent housing advice and a free contract checking service.

living at home

Many student choose to live at home while studying. The student union runs tailored support service if you are living home to make sure you get the most out of University and the same opportunities to socialise with others living on campus.

General Packing list for the University

Tips and Tricks

    Don't leave packing until the last minute – it takes longer than you think! Check what your halls provide first – you don't want to end up with double of everything. Chances are your new bedroom won't be too big, and your storage space won't be either. Pack sensibly and remember you'll be moving out again in June so it's not really that long. Buy second hand! I found all my cutlery and crockery in charity shops which saved me SO much money, and my second hand John Lewis finds were far better quality than I would have ever got otherwise.

Okay, now onto the actual packing list! I'm not going to be talking about the obvious things like clothes, toiletries or your phone – hopefully you know to bring them anyway. This is more for the extras which are just as important but may not immediately come to mind…

Electronics

  • Chargers!.
  • Extension cable – this one is more useful than you would think.
  • Headphones
  • USB memory stick
  • Speakers

Stationary

  • Books and notepads can be pretty bulky, so try and get what you can at Uni.
  • Planner – oh my goodness my life would have fallen apart without this one of these. Highly recommend!
  • Don't spend too much money on pens and stationary – you'll go pens like nobody's business so buy in bulk or buy cheap.
  • I saved all my stationary buying until mid/late September, meaning I was able to buy all my folders and notepads on sale after the mad 'back to school' rush was over. Just a money saving tip!

Kitchen Supplies

  • See what you're able to share with housemates. They may be willing to share pots, pans etc and this will save money and space.
  • Scissors
  • Pizza cutter – you might find this becomes pretty regularly used…
  • Tupperware
  • Oven gloves
  • Tea Towels
  • Bottle openers and tin openers

Bedroom

  • Hangers
  • Laundry basket
  • Small first aid kit full of plasters, paracetamol and the likes, just in case.
  • Fun room decor. Make the place your own! For room inspo, read

Miscellaneous

  • Umbrella. Trust me you'll need one in Manchester.
  • If you have room to bring a bike up, do! It makes travelling so much cheaper and quicker.
  • Small sewing kit
  • A weekend or 'home' bag
  • A reusable water bottle
  • SHOWER SHOES..
