Mathematics ON1

From the understanding of subsets to proving statements through mathematical induction the purpose of this course unit is to introduce students to the linguistics of Mathematics, which includes the use of English in Mathematics. The content covered is as follows:

  1. Arrangements for the Course
  2. Sets
  3. Subsets - Finite and Infinite Sets
  4. Operations on Sets
  5. Boolean Algebra
  6. Set Theory
  7. Propositional Logic
  8. Logically equivalent statements
  9. Predicate Logic
  10. Quantifiers
  11. Logical equivalences
  12. Inequalities
  13. Operations over Inequalities
  14. Methods of Proof
  15. Proof by contradiction
  16. Harmonic, geometric and arithmetic means
  17. Inequalities in single variable
  18. Linear Inequalities in two variables
  19. Convexity
    20.

ON1 follows a weekly content basis where all learning resources are provided online via Blackboard. The unit mark is divided between online coursework quizzes for certain weeks and a final exam took about the end of the first semester.

What is achieved from completion:

  • Familiarity with inequalities and with sets and logic
  • The ability to carry out routine operations involving the topics in the syllabus
  • Be acquainted with tools and methods that can be applied in the courses given in the host department or subsequent years
    •

ON1 allows students to analyze the branch of mathematics that analyses how mathematics is expressed in different linguistic formats through mathematical logic, proof, and other methods. This may be different from what you have learned for A-levels so I do recommend you spend time and remember to take your time and think hard before you answer these questions.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Lectures - 24
  • Tutorials - 11
  • Independent study - 65
