Academic Skills

The purpose of this unit is to enable students to learn and practice key skills that are needed to operate effectively as a student at the University of Manchester. This unit like ICT is different in the sense that it doesn't consist of a final exam as it is purely based on the amount of coursework a student completes. Here are some of the things you may expect to come across for this unit:

  1. Presentations (both individually and as a group)
  2. Group work
  3. Research skills (identifying, reading and evaluating academic sources)
  4. Academic writing (including referencing appropriately)
  5. Self reflection
    6.

For this unit, I would recommend students learn how to reference using the Manchester Harvard Referencing Guide to get accustomed to the type of reference that is expected at the University of Manchester.

What is achieved from completion:

  • Analytical skills
  • Group/team working
  • Leadership
  • Oral communcation
  • Problem solving
  • Research
  • Written communication
    •

Academic skills enable students to grasp management and operational skills that are necessary for a work environment and show how academic journaling is conducted. In the long term, this will help the student to become more perceptive of what information is valuable and what is not which is needed when conducting huge piles of research on certain documentation.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • External visits - 1
  • Lectures - 5
  • Practical classes & workshops - 7
  • Tutorials - 22
  • Independent study - 65
