Physics 2 - World of the Electrons

This unit shows all the physics behind one of the most fascinating constituents of the atom which is the electrons. Here are some of the things to expect with this course unit:

  1. Electric Charge, Forces and Fields
  2. Electrical Potential Energy and Potential
  3. Capacitors & Dielectrics
  4. Electric Current, Resistance, Energy and Power
  5. DC Circuits & Kirchhoff's Laws
  6. Magnets and Magnetic Fields
  7. Superconductors and Magnetic Fields Around Conductors
  8. Magnetic Force On Moving Charges
  9. Electromagnetic Induction
  10. More Electromagnetic Induction
  11. Physics Of The Atom
  12. Semiconductors
    13.

This unit follows a weekly content basis. The work posted each week must be completed for all tutorial sessions. The unit mark is divided between online coursework quizzes for certain weeks and a final exam is taken at the end of the first semester. If a student requires aid with a task or has any questions regarding the unit or content of a given week they can attend drop-in sessions to clarify or answer this query.

What is achieved from completion:

  • An understanding of simple DC circuits consisting of capacitors or resistors under steady-state conditions
  • A grasp of the behavior of moving charges or current-carrying conductors in magnetic fields
  • Appreciation for the band theory of solids to explain properties of conductors, insulators, and semiconductors and the role of doping in simple semiconductor devices (including the p-n junction)
  • An understanding of the consequences and implications of electromagnetic induction
  • An understanding of Bohr's atomic model
  • An understanding of the properties and interaction of electrically charged

Physics 2 covers the nature of atoms' subatomic particles the electron. The tasks that students carry out helps improves the students' ability to use maths in context and develop the student's reasoning and critical thinking.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Assesment written exam - 2
  • Lectures - 24
  • Tutorials - 11
  • Independent study - 63
