Computational Thinking

Have you ever baked a cake? You likely had to follow a certain procedure that allows you to create your desired cake. Computational Thinking is all about understanding a problem and creating algorithms that help solve the problem. Here is what you can expect to come across in this unit:

  1. Decomposition of a problem into a series of ordered steps
  2. Techniques such as decomposition, abstraction and generalisation to problems
  3. Descriptions of computational complexity in an informal way
  4. Self-referential problems relating to the limits of computation
    5.

"Children must be taught how to think not what to think" - Margaret Mead. It is important to use Computational Thinking in all aspects of our lives not only academically. This field of study opens many paths as it supports problem-solving across all disciplines from maths to humanities.

What is achieved from completion:

  • Pattern recognition
  • Pattern abstraction
  • Algorithm design
  • Decomposition
    •

These skills in the long term build a student's work ethic and force the student to become more adaptable through individual and group tasks which helps increase the employability standard of that student.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Lectures - 10
  • Practical classes & workshops - 12
  • Independent study - 100
