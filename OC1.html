Mathematics OC1

This unit aims to show students the most basic principles of calculus and algebra.

  1. Define the exponential function and apply the rules of indices to simplify algebraic expressions
  2. Use the definition of the logarithm, together with its rules, to solve logarithmic equations
  3. Identify and solve quadratic equations using the quadratic formula
  4. Determine the equation of a line given its gradient and a point through which it passes
  5. Calculate the gradient of a line given: (a) two points it passes through; (b) the gradient of a line to which it is parallel/perpendicular
  6. Find the coordinates of the intersection points of two curves
  7. Write down the equation of a tangent to a curve at a point
  8. Given two points in the plane, determine the equation of a circle centred at one point and passing through the other
  9. Define the domain of a function and calculate its inverse
  10. Determine and simplify the composition of two functions
  11. Convert angles between degrees and radians
  12. Using the unit circle, recall the definition of the trigonometric functions, and apply this to determine the values of these functions at commonly-encountered angles
  13. Find the size of an angle using the inverse trigonometric functions together with geometric reasoning
  14. Use trigonometric identities to determine all angles and side-lengths in a right-angled triangle, given a side-length and one other piece of information (side-length or angle)
  15. Use the chain/product/quotient rules to differentiate the composition/product/quotient of two functions
  16. Apply the rules for differentiation to determine the coordinates of, and classify, the stationary points of a given function
  17. Use integration to find the area between two curves

OC1 follows a weekly content basis where all learning resources are provided online via Blackboard. This unit is for beginner maths students determined by a Mathematic Diagnostic Test at the beginning of the first semester. The unit mark is divided between online coursework quizzes for certain weeks and a final exam took about the end of the first semester.

What is achieved from completion:

  • The tools necessary to advance to more complex mathematics
  • An appreciation for fundamental mathematical techniques
  • A new outlook or perspective in a specific area of mathematics
    •

OC1 is more tuned for students are new to concepts of A-level mathematics.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Lectures - 24
  • Tutorials - 12
  • Independent study - 64
