Mathematics OD2

The main objective of this course unit is to teach you various, mostly visual, mathematical concepts ranging from Interpolation to Areas, Lengths, and Volumes. The course unit is divided into 2 main aspects that contribute to the weighting of the final coursework mark which are:

  • Theoretical Part
  • Coding Part
    •

Theoretical Part:

  1. Interpolation
  2. Numerical Differentiation
  3. Numerical Integration
  4. Polar Coordinates
  5. Numerical Solutions of Equations
  6. Area, Lenghts and Volumes
  7. Recurrence Relations & Reduction Formulae
  8. Integration by Parts
    9.

Coding Part (Wolfram Mathematica):

  1. Basic arithmetic
  2. Variables
  3. Brackets
  4. Basic Functions
  5. Simplification
  6. Solution of Equations
  7. Differentiation
  8. Integration
  9. Graphics
  10. Assigning values to variables
  11. Defining Functions
  12. Plotting Points
  13. Linear Interpolation
  14. Numerical Differentiation
  15. Numerical Integration
  16. Polar Coordinates
  17. Polar Curves
  18. Numerical Root Finding
  19. Manipulate
  20. Areas, Lengths and Volumes: Polar Coordinates
  21. Areas, Lengths and Volumes: Cartesian Coordinates
    22.

OD2 follows a weekly content basis where all learning resources are provided online via Blackboard. The unit mark is divided between online coursework quizzes for certain weeks and a final exam took about the end of the second semester. The coding part is all done on Wolfram Mathematica and makes part of your coursework mark.

What is achieved from completion:

  • The ability to calculate interpolating functions and values using linear and quadratic interpolation formulae and their inverses
  • The ability to select and apply numerical differentiation formulae to approximate derivatives, and discuss the accuracy of these approximations
  • The ability to approximate integrals using the trapezium rule and Simpson's rule, and discuss the accuracy of these approxiamtions
  • The ability to scale and rotate the shape by multiplying the radius calue and adding a constant to theta, respectively
  • The ability to derive and apply reduction formulae to evaluate definite and indefinite integrals
    •

OD2 is one of the units that has content that differs from A-levels. I would recommend downloading and trying out Wolfram Mathematica for yourself to be ready for the coding part of the course unit.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Lectures - 24
  • Tutorials - 11
  • Independent study - 65
