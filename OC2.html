Chemistry 1

OC2 is a further extension of OC1 with the content to some extent being the same as OB2 but mostly content that is seen in OB1. Here is what you can expect to see in this unit:

  1. Complex Numbers
  2. Sequences and Series
  3. Further Differentiation
  4. Further Integration
  5. Rational Functions and Partial Fractions
  6. Integration using Partial Fractions
    7.

OC2 follows a weekly content basis where all learning resources are provided online via Blackboard. The unit mark is divided between online coursework quizzes for certain weeks and a final exam took about the end of the second semester.

What is achieved from completion:

  • The ability to perform arithmetic operations on complex numbers and compute their moduli, arhuments and conjugates
  • The ability to write Taylor and Maclaurin Series and apply them to compute limits
  • The ability to write integration by parts and integration by substitution formulae and apply them in integration exercises
  • The ability to apply the algorithms of simplifying improper rational functions to compute their integrals
    •

OC2 takes more content chunks from OB1 so make sure that you have a firm grasp on all of the content you learnt from OC1 a it will be very important to achieve progress in this unit.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Lectures - 22
  • Tutorials - 11
  • Independent study - 67
