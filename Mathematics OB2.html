Mathematics OB2

OB2 is nothing more than an extension of OB1 if you are taking it, with the content going further into detail about some previous concepts and also taking a look at new learning fields.

  1. Complex numbers
  2. Matrices
  3. Ordinary Differential Equations (ODEs)
  4. Partial differentiation
    5.

OB2 follows a weekly content basis where all learning resources are provided online via Blackboard. The unit mark is divided between online coursework quizzes for certain weeks and a final exam is taken at the end of the second semester.

What is achieved from completion:

  • The ability to carry out arithmetic operations on complex numbers and represent results in cartesian, polar, and exponential forms as well as on an Argand Diagram.
  • The ability to solve and analyze systems of linear equations using matrices
  • The ability to calculate improper integrals and analyze functions that are defined by integrals and analyze functions that are defined by integrals
  • The ability to solve homogenous, separable, and linear first-order Ordinary Differential Equations and analyze their properties using direction fields
  • The ability to carry out partial differentiation and use the chain rule to estimate errors in functions.
    •

The content in OB2 may be a bit different from the content that was learned at A-levels so don't let your eye off this one but if you are an avid mathematician enthusiast you should love this unit as there is so much more to learn.

The recommended study hours for this unit is:

  • Lectures - 24
  • Tutorials - 11
  • Independent study - 65
